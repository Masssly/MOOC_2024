About the Wikidata MOOC

Wikidata is becoming a central point in the web of data and one of the most active Wikimedia projects. This knowledgebase remains difficult for newcomers to understand. That's why Wikimedia France has designed this free-access MOOC, which will teach you how to contribute independently to the Wikidata knowledgebase. Divided into 5 chapters and containing several parts, it covers general knowledge about databases, queries, data openness, and the semantic web initially. The functioning of Wikidata and the principles on which it is based are explained to you next, before inviting you to contribute.

You will discover how to create new data pages on Wikidata, which topics belong there, and how data quality is ensured. You will explore the possibilities of re-using Wikidata's data. You will learn how to interact with a community of several thousand volunteers.

The first edition of this MOOC took place in French from April 26th to July 7th, 2022.

The second edition of this MOOC of this MOOC also in French took place from September 23rd to December 31st, 2023.

This MOOC is intended for anyone interested in the Wikidata knowledgebase, specifically students, as well as library, gallery, archive, and museum (GLAM) professionals.

Prerequisites

A stable internet connection is necessary. Some parts of this MOOC can be accessed on a mobile phone, but exercises and assessments typically require opening a new tab in your browser, and therefore navigating between these two tabs.

Course Format and Assessment

The Wikidata MOOC is open for 5 weeks. It consists of 5 chapters. One or more assessment exercises are provided with each chapter. For the final evaluation, you will create your own Items on Wikidata and will be assessed by your peers (other participants enrolled in the Wikidata MOOC)

You will be able to interact directly on Wikidata, allowing you to easily engage with the community.

Pedagogical Team of the Wikidata MOOC

This MOOC was created by the Wikimedia France association, with financial support from Wikimedia Deutschland (Wikimedia Germany). About ten people participated in its creation. Six Wikimedians will present key concepts of Wikidata to you.

A part of the Wikidata MOOC team during the filming of videos at the Wikimedia France offices on Martel Street in Paris.

Amélie Charles

Amélie, who is the distance learning training manager for Wikimedia France, is trained in instructional design. She designed the majority of the content for this MOOC and then ensured its integration onto the current training platform.

Rémy Gerbet

Rémy Gerbet, the executive director of Wikimedia France, will explain what Wikimedia projects are and summarize the history of Wikidata.

Florian Cuny

Florian Cuny, a computer science student, presents here general concepts about data, databases, queries, open data, and the semantic web.

Capucine-Marin Dubroca-Voisin

Capucine Marin, President of the Wikimedia France association, will present the different types of pages on Wikidata, the anatomy of an item page, and the creation of a new personal account on Wikidata.

Georges Fodouop

Frequently Asked Questions

Which browser should I use?

This MOOC will work best with a Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari browser.

You can also view the full list ofcompatible browsers.

What prior knowledge is required?

You only need to know how to navigate the internet. No prior training in Knowledgebase is required to start this MOOC.

How long does the MOOC last?

It depends on your prior knowledge. This MOOC opens on April 22, 2024, and will remain open until May 31, 2024. Learning is not divided by weeks; you progress at your own pace. However, assessments must be submitted before the end of May.

Is it really free?

Yes, indeed! No hidden fees in this MOOC, no pop-up windows will ask you for money.
